Scientific Advisory Board

Joost-Pieter Katoen

Software Modeling and Verification Expert

Joost-Pieter Katoen is a Dutch theoretical computer scientist based in Germany. He is distinguished professor in Computer Science and head of the Software Modeling and Verification Group at RWTH Aachen University. Furthermore, he is part-time associated to the Formal Methods & Tools group at the University of Twente. Katoen's main research interests are formal methods, computer aided verification, in particular model checking, concurrency theory, and semantics, in particular semantics of probabilistic programming languages. His research is largely tool and application oriented. Together with Christel Baier he wrote and published the book Principles of Model Checking.

Dr. Imran Khan

Molecular Biotechnology Expert

Dr. Imran Khan received PhD fellowship from German Academic and Exchange Service (DAAD) for his doctoral study at Institute for Molecular Biotechnology, RWTH Aachen University, Germany. He also served Vienna Institute for Biotechnology, BOKU, Vienna, Austria, as researcher where he investigated expression and targeting of recombinant proteins in plant tissues. He also received research funding as PI and supervised PhD and master students working as Assistant Professor at Center for Interdisciplinary Research, Islamabad, Pakistan. In 2021, he joined University of California, Davis, USA, as Project Scientist. He is currently working on highly interdisciplinary project for development of platform technologies for bio-manufacturing on Mars to provide food and pharmaceuticals for colonists, funded by National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), USA.

Prof. Dr. Marielle Stoelinga

Software Modeling and Verification Expert

Marielle Stoelinga is a Dutch Professor in IT Risk Management at FMT group, University of Twente, and Dept. of Software Science, Radboud University, the Netherlands. She leads a research team that develops quantitative risk assessments methods: techniques to analyze, predict and improve the reliability of complex systems, e.g. via fault trees, model-based testing, and reliability modeling through stochastic model-checking. Predictive maintenance, human factors and the interplay between safety and security are her special interests. She is the programme leader of executive Master on Risk Management, and holds advisory roles at Schouten & Nelissen University of Applied Sciences, and Nyenrode University. She holds a prestigious ERC consolidator grant on the integration of Safety and Security risks. She leads a large national consortium on Predictive Maintenance, funded by the Dutch National Research Agenda NWA.
